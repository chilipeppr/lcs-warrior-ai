# LCS STRIVE Center — Innovation & Design Lab

The STRIVE Center is Liberty Christian School's 4,000-square-foot innovation lab. STRIVE stands for **S**cience, **T**echnology, **R**esearch, **I**nnovation, **V**irtual Learning, and **E**ntrepreneurship.

**Director:** Jamie Michalek (Innovation and Design Center Director)
**Opened:** April 2023
**Location:** LCS campus, Argyle, TX
**Serves:** All students, preschool through 12th grade

## Mission
"Get all students into this space throughout the school year." The STRIVE Center takes concepts from theory to practice — students learn by building, failing, iterating, and shipping real projects.

## Sub-Programs

### 3D Print Lab (Bambu Lab)
- 3D printing stations with Bambu Lab printers
- CadQuery (Python) parametric CAD
- Design-for-manufacturing curriculum
- Student creations: nameplates, keychains, phone stands, robot parts, functional prototypes

### Robotics (Intel OpenBot)
- Open-source self-driving robots (~$50/robot + old Android phone)
- 3D-printed chassis on Bambu Lab printers
- Arduino + motors + ultrasonic sensors + wheel encoders
- Machine learning: students train neural networks in Jupyter notebooks
- Hallway races and competition days

### Electronics (Adom InstaPCB)
- PCB design curriculum using KiCad
- Circuit simulation and schematic capture
- Adom InstaPCB fabrication — 4-hour turnaround from design to physical board
- Students design, fabricate, and assemble real PCBs
- Component sourcing via Mouser (40-min drone delivery to Fort Worth)
- Most advanced high school electronics program in the country

### AI & Machine Learning
- 18-week Applied AI Engineering curriculum
- Claude Code as the AI backbone
- Docker containers per student ("laptop in the cloud")
- Neural network training for self-driving robots
- Computer vision, sensor fusion, data science

### Entrepreneurship
- Conrad Challenge competition preparation
- Business plan development
- Local business mentorship partnerships
- Internship program
- Real-world project-based learning

## Equipment
- Bambu Lab 3D printers (PLA, multiple colors)
- Power tools and hand tools
- CAD software (CadQuery, Fusion 360, Bambu Studio)
- Intel OpenBot kits (Arduino, motors, sensors)
- Old Android phones (donated, used as robot brains/cameras)
- Soldering stations (for electronics sub-program)
- KiCad + Adom platform (for PCB design)

## Competitions
- Conrad Challenge (innovation/entrepreneurship)
- TAPPS Academic competitions
- Internal: Robot Olympics, 3D Print Showcase, AI Competition Day

## The Vision
The STRIVE Center + Adom platform positions Liberty Christian as the most advanced high school STEM program in the country. Students don't just learn theory — they design PCBs that get fabricated in a real factory, train ML models that drive physical robots, and build apps that deploy to a school-wide platform. This is college-level engineering delivered to high schoolers.
